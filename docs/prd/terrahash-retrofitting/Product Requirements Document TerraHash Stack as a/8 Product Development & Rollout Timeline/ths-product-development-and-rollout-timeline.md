# TerraHash Stack as a Service: Product Development & Rollout Timeline

## Executive Overview

This document outlines the comprehensive product development and service rollout plan for TerraHash Stack as a Service over a 36-month horizon (Q4 2025 - Q4 2028). The timeline is structured around four critical phases: **Foundation** (Months 1-6), **Scale** (Months 7-12), **Expansion** (Months 13-24), and **Optimization** (Months 25-36), with each phase building upon previous capabilities while introducing new service modules and market segments.

The rollout strategy balances aggressive growth targets with operational risk management, ensuring each phase delivers measurable value to customers while establishing the foundation for subsequent expansion.

## PHASE 1: FOUNDATION (Months 1-6, Q4 2025 - Q1 2026)

**Primary Goal:** Establish TerraHash Stack as a Service with proven MVP (Minimum Viable Product) delivering core retrofit and support services to early adopters.

### Month 13-15: Service Portfolio Expansion

**New Service Lines:**

**Heat Recovery as a Service (HRaaS):**

- **Business Model:** End-to-end heat recovery deployment, customer pays only for heat delivered
- **Revenue Stream:** $15-25/MMBtu delivered heat (vs. one-time infrastructure sales)
- **Target Applications:** Greenhouse operations, lumber drying, industrial processes, district heating
- **Geographic Focus:** Rural mining locations with agricultural/industrial offtake opportunities
- **Pilot Deployment:** 5-8 facilities with established offtake agreements

**Mining Fleet Management (MaaS - Mining as a Service):**

- **Full Facility Operations:** TerraHash operates customer facility under revenue-share agreement
- **Target Customers:** Energy producers, REITs, non-technical mining investors
- **Service Scope:** 24/7 operations, maintenance, pool management, treasury optimization
- **Revenue Model:** 15-25% of gross mining revenue (vs. fixed SLA fees)
- **Risk Profile:** TerraHash assumes operational risk, customer guaranteed minimum returns

**Immersion Cooling Retrofit:**

- **Technology:** Standardize Fog Hashing C6 immersion tanks for older equipment (S17, T17 series)
- **Cost Advantage:** $280-380K/MW vs. $500-650K/MW for direct-to-chip liquid cooling
- **Use Cases:** Legacy equipment life extension, extreme heat environments, noise-sensitive locations
- **Integration:** AI platform supports both liquid cooling and immersion under unified management
