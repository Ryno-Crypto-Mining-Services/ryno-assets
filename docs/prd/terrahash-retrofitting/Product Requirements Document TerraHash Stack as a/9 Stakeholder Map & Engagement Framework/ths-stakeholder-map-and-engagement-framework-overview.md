## **PART 3: EXTERNAL STAKEHOLDERS - CUSTOMERS**

**Three Customer Segments:**

**Segment 1: Regional Operators (1-10 MW)**

- Profile: Independent, founder-led, single/few facilities, limited expertise
- Annual revenue: $5-15M, capex budget: $1-5M
- Decision maker: Owner/CEO, Operations Manager
- Needs: Simplicity, support, financing options
- TerraHash approach: Gold SLA ($5K/MW/month), project excellence, proactive account management
- Engagement: Monthly during retrofit, quarterly business reviews, on-demand support

**Segment 2: Institutional Miners (10-50 MW)**

- Profile: VC/PE-backed, multiple facilities, professional operations
- Annual revenue: $30-150M, capex budget: $10-30M
- Decision maker: CEO, COO, VP Operations
- Needs: Scalability, integration, reporting
- TerraHash approach: Platinum SLA ($10K/MW/month), enterprise integrations, dedicated account manager
- Engagement: Monthly ops review, quarterly business reviews, annual executive review

**Segment 3: Energy Producers (5-50 MW co-location)**

- Profile: Utilities, natural gas, renewable energy companies, limited mining expertise
- Annual revenue: $100M+, capex budget: $50M+
- Decision maker: Chief Development Officer, external consultants
- Needs: Turnkey operation, energy monetization, risk mitigation
- TerraHash approach: Mining as a Service (full facility management, revenue share), heat recovery
- Engagement: Weekly ops (first 90 days), monthly business review, quarterly executive

**Customer Success Engagement Model:**

- Onboarding (30 days): Project handoff, SLA education, platform training, performance validation
- Growth Phase (Months 2-12): Monthly SLA reporting, optimization recommendations, expansion identification
- Expansion Phase (Year 2+): Multi-site portfolio management, tier upgrades, module expansion, reference development

## **PART 5: EXTERNAL STAKEHOLDERS - REGULATORS & GOVERNMENT**

**Federal Regulators (U.S.):**

1. **SEC (Securities & Exchange Commission)**
    - Jurisdiction: If TerraHash becomes public or raises institutional capital
    - Regulations: Regulation D, Securities Act, Exchange Act
    - Engagement: Compliance monitoring, securities counsel for fundraising, annual review
2. **CFTC (Commodity Futures Trading Commission)**
    - Jurisdiction: If offering BTC futures or derivatives
    - Regulations: Dodd-Frank, binary options, BTC product regulations
    - Engagement: Monitor developments, external counsel if needed
3. **EPA (Environmental Protection Agency)**
    - Jurisdiction: Energy efficiency, emissions
    - Regulations: Energy Star, carbon reporting
    - Engagement: Energy tracking, sustainability reporting, industry participation
4. **DOE (Department of Energy)**
    - Jurisdiction: Energy consumption reporting (proposed), efficiency incentives
    - Engagement: Monitor rulemaking, build compliance reporting, track incentives

**State Regulators:**

1. **Texas PUC (Public Utilities Commission)**
    - Jurisdiction: ERCOT market, grid interconnection
    - Engagement: Monitor ERCOT rules, coordinate demand response, ensure compliance
2. **New York DEC (Department of Environmental Conservation)**
    - Current: 2-year PoW moratorium (expires ~2027)
    - Engagement: Monitor for changes, prepare for post-moratorium entry
3. **Wyoming Business Council**
    - Programs: Blockchain mining tax incentives, renewable integration
    - Engagement: Maintain relationships, participate in forums, leverage incentives

**International Regulators (Phase 3+ expansion):**

1. **Canada: Ministry of Natural Resources & Forestry**
    - Jurisdiction: Mining in Canada
    - Engagement: Early market research, local consultants, environmental assessments
2. **EU: European Commission**
    - Key regulations: GDPR, energy efficiency, MiCA
    - Engagement: International legal counsel, compliance program, data residency

## **PART 7: CONFLICT RESOLUTION FRAMEWORK**

**Conflict Type 1: Customer vs. Internal Operations**

- Example: Customer demands 99.9% uptime, operations uncertain
- Resolution: Escalate to VP Customer Success & COO; define realistic SLA with penalties
- Prevention: Accurate capability forecasting, conservative commitments, continuous improvement

**Conflict Type 2: Vendor vs. Customer**

- Example: Chilldyne equipment failure impacts customer
- Resolution: Escalate to CTO & COO; coordinate replacement; customer communication
- Prevention: Quality assurance, redundancy, backup suppliers

**Conflict Type 3: Partner vs. Internal Strategy**

- Example: Braiins wants higher dev fees
- Resolution: Escalate to CEO & CTO; find mutual solution (volume commitments for pricing)
- Prevention: Regular strategic planning, transparent communication

**Conflict Type 4: Regulatory vs. Business Operations**

- Example: New energy reporting requirements
- Resolution: Escalate to General Counsel & CFO; assess compliance cost
- Prevention: Regulatory monitoring, compliance-ready design, government relations
