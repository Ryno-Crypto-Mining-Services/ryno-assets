## Executive Overview

Successful execution of TerraHash Stack as a Service requires systematic alignment and engagement across a diverse ecosystem of **internal stakeholders** (company team), **external partners** (strategic co-investors and technology partners), **customers** (mining operators across multiple segments), **vendors** (equipment suppliers and infrastructure providers), and **regulators** (government agencies and compliance bodies). This document maps all key stakeholders, defines their interests and dependencies, establishes engagement protocols, and identifies critical success factors for stakeholder relationships.

## PART 7: STAKEHOLDER CONFLICT RESOLUTION

### Stakeholder Conflict Management Process

**Conflict Type 1: Customer vs. Internal Operations**

- Example: Customer demands 99.9% uptime SLA, but operations team uncertain about delivery capability
- **Resolution:** Escalate to VP Customer Success and COO; define realistic SLA with clear penalties, set aggressive but achievable targets
- **Prevention:** Accurate capability forecasting, conservative SLA commitments, continuous improvement focus

**Conflict Type 2: Vendor vs. Customer**

- Example: Chilldyne equipment failure impacts customer facility performance
- **Resolution:** Escalate to CTO and COO; coordinate with Chilldyne on replacement; customer communication from VP Customer Success
- **Prevention:** Quality assurance processes, redundant equipment, backup suppliers

**Conflict Type 3: Partner vs. Internal Strategy**

- Example: Braiins wants to increase dev fees; TerraHash wants to reduce cost structure
- **Resolution:** Escalate to CEO and CTO; find mutually beneficial solution (volume commitments for pricing reduction)
- **Prevention:** Regular strategic planning sessions, transparent communication of business drivers

**Conflict Type 4: Regulatory vs. Business Operations**

- Example: New state energy reporting requirements add compliance burden
- **Resolution:** Escalate to General Counsel and CFO; assess compliance cost, build into business model
- **Prevention:** Regulatory monitoring, compliance-ready platform design, government relations
